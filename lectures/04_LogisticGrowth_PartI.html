Meet the Squirlicorn (Sciurus monocerus})

Natural History

  • Extremely territorial: Strictly ONE squirlicorn per oak tree.
  • One colonist appears on Oakie Island, which contains \(K\) oak trees
  • Reproduces asexually with some fixed probability \(p_b\) per year.
  • Offspring disperses to nearest tree.
  • If tree is occupied, existing squirlicorn assassinates newcomer with its horn.
  • Is (of course) immortal.

On Oakie Island: 64 trees and 0.2 offspring / year

Logistic growth ...

Some math

\[E(N_{t+1}) = N_t + b \, N_t \, T_a\] \(T_a\) = available trees, \(b\) - is birth rate.

Remember \(E()\) is expectation (NOT a variable just a way to accept that were talking about random variables and are interested in their MEAN behavior but well drop it from here on out).

Key calculation: \[T_a = K - N_t\] \[N_{t+1} = N_t + b N_t(K - N_t)\] \[N_{t+1} - N_t = b N_t (K - N_t)\] Or - more generally with a death rate: \(\lambda = b - d\)

\[\Delta N_t = \lambda N_t (K - N_t)\]

Lets just squeeze time to make this a differential equation:

\[{N_{t+\Delta t} - N_t \over \Delta t} = {r_b\over \Delta t} N_t (K - N_t)\] \[{dN \over dt} = r_0 N_t (K - N_t)\]

This is the logistic population growth model.

Properties and Assumptions

\(\Delta N = \lambda()\) \({dN \over dt} = r N_t (K - N_t)\)

The key assumption is that growth rate decreases linearly with population.

  • At \(N = 0\) - growth = \(r_0\)
  • At \(N = K\) - growth = \(0\)
  • At \(N > K\) - growth < \(0\)

Density Dependence

Any relationship where \(r\) or \(\lambda\) depends on \(N\) is called Density Dependence.
